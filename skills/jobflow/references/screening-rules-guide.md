# Screening Rules Guide

Screening rules should be written for one user profile and stored in `screening_rules.md`.

## Recommended Sections

- Candidate profile
- Target roles
- Priority order
- Must-apply signals
- Location preference
- Company preference
- Exclusions
- Scoring heuristic

## Positive Signals

Examples:

- Web3 growth, wallet growth, onboarding, lifecycle, referral, task systems, airdrop or reward incentives
- SEO product, SEO growth, programmatic SEO, AEO, content growth, organic traffic, website growth
- internet growth product, lifecycle growth, activation, retention, reactivation, conversion
- AI productization, AI Agent, AI tools, content/productivity AI, data-driven product iteration
- platform, ecosystem, creator, content, social, commercialization, or marketplace product

## Exclusion Signals

Examples:

- AML, anti-money-laundering, KYC/KYB-heavy compliance
- transaction monitoring, sanctions screening, suspicious transaction reporting, financial crime
- Web3 deep trading product: matching engine, order flow, derivatives, leverage, quant strategy, market making, trading risk
- hardware-only, medical clinical, QA, pure sales, pure customer service, internship, junior, low-salary roles

## Scoring Heuristic

Use a simple practical score:

- Strong match: must-apply signal plus no exclusion and good location/salary fit.
- Good match: target role with several positive signals and no exclusion.
- Backup: generic product role with acceptable salary/location and no obvious mismatch.
- Skip: any exclusion signal or obvious mismatch.

When uncertain, prefer recording `needs_review` rather than applying automatically.
