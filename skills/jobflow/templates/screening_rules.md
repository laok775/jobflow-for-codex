# Job Search Screening Rules

Last updated: YYYY-MM-DD

## Candidate Profile

- Describe the user's seniority, domain experience, strengths, and constraints.

## Target Roles

- Product Manager
- Growth Product Manager
- AI Product Manager

## Priority Order

1. Highest priority target roles and domains.
2. Good secondary matches.
3. Backup roles.

## Must-Apply Signals

- SEO product, SEO growth, programmatic SEO, AEO, content growth, organic traffic.
- AI productization, AI Agent, AI tools, content/productivity AI.
- Lifecycle growth, activation, retention, reactivation, conversion, referral.

## Location Preference

- Remote first for globally distributed roles.
- Local city preference for internet/non-remote roles.

## Company Preference

- Startups and small companies are acceptable when the role matches strongly.
- Large companies can be deprioritized if the user has a clear reason.

## Exclusions

- AML, anti-money-laundering, KYC/KYB-heavy compliance, transaction monitoring, sanctions screening, financial crime.
- Deep Web3 trading product: matching engine, order flow, derivatives, leverage, quant strategy, market making, trading risk.
- Hardware-only, medical clinical, QA, internship, junior, low-salary, pure sales, pure customer service.

## Scoring Heuristic

- Strong match: must-apply signal plus no exclusion and good location/salary fit.
- Good match: target role with several positive signals and no exclusion.
- Backup: generic product role with acceptable salary/location and no obvious mismatch.
- Skip: any exclusion signal or obvious mismatch.
